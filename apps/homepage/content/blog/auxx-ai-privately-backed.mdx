Auxx.ai is privately backed with $2M to build an AI-native CRM and helpdesk that small businesses can actually afford.

I wanted to write this down because the question comes up enough that it's worth being direct about.

## Why we took capital at all

The surface area we're building is wider than most people realize. Auxx.ai is a support inbox, a CRM, a workflow engine, a knowledge base, a multi-provider AI layer, and a set of first-party integrations (Gmail, Outlook, Shopify, Stripe, live chat, SMS, and more) — all in one product.

A single-founder bootstrapped shop can ship a few of those well. It cannot ship all of them at the quality small businesses actually need. Capital lets us hire engineers, accelerate the open-source release, and keep pricing low enough that a one-person Shopify store can run the same stack a 30-person support team runs.

## What we're actually building

The shortest possible version:

**An AI-native CRM for small businesses.** Helpdesk, CRM, workflow automation, messaging, and knowledge base in one product. Open source at the core, self-hostable, and priced so a solo founder and a 50-person support team can both run it.

A few things we care about more than most:

- **Open source.** The core platform is open. We want people to be able to fork it, self-host it, audit it, and extend it. That is a product decision, not a marketing decision.
- **Multi-provider AI.** We don't bet the product on a single model vendor. Bring your own OpenAI, Anthropic, Google, or local model — the AI layer is provider-agnostic.
- **Not Shopify-only.** Auxx.ai works with any email (Gmail, Outlook, IMAP) and any commerce stack. Shopify is one integration among many, not the whole identity of the product.
- **Built for small businesses first.** Every feature has to make sense for a one-person shop before it makes sense for a 500-person company. Most CRMs are built the other way around, and small businesses pay the tax.

## What's next

The next six months, concretely:

1. **GA launch.** Auxx.ai comes out of invite-only access.
3. **Platform depth.** Workflows, datasets, and Kopilot (our AI assistant) all get meaningful upgrades. More integrations. Better reporting.
4. **Team.** We're hiring. If you want to work on open-source infrastructure for small businesses, reach out.

## The long version

Small businesses have been told for years that they need to choose between cheap tools that don't work and expensive tools built for the enterprise. That's a false choice, and AI is finally the wedge that makes it possible to ship something that's both.

That is the bet. The capital just buys us enough runway to make it real.
