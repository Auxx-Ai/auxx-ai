## The two-subscription problem

Freshworks is a publicly traded company (FRSH) with over 50,000 customers, founded in 2010 in Chennai and now headquartered in San Mateo. They make solid products. But if you run a Shopify store and need both customer support and a CRM, you're signing up for two separate subscriptions.

**Freshdesk** handles support tickets. **Freshsales** handles your CRM. They're different products with different billing, different dashboards, and different data stores. Want AI on top? That's a third line item.

For a 5-person Shopify support team on Pro plans with AI, you're looking at roughly **$615/month** before you've even started customizing anything.

## What Freshworks does well

Credit where it's due. Freshworks has been building support software for over 15 years and it shows in a few areas.

### Mature product suite

Freshdesk is a battle-tested helpdesk. It handles high-volume ticket routing, SLA management, and multi-brand support. If you've used Zendesk or Help Scout, the interface will feel familiar. The ecosystem is wide — marketplace apps, integrations, and a developer platform.

### Omnichannel options

If you pay for Freshdesk Omni, you get email, chat, phone, and social channels in one view. The unified inbox works. Agents can switch between channels without losing context on a conversation.

### Decent entry pricing

Freshdesk's Growth plan starts at **$19/agent/month**, which is reasonable for basic ticketing. If all you need is a shared inbox with some automation rules, it's a fair deal. Freshsales also has a free tier for up to 3 users, which works for very small teams testing the waters.

### Freddy AI capabilities

Freddy AI has improved significantly. It offers agentic workflows, a copilot for agents, and auto-triage. The technology itself is solid — when you can afford to turn it on.

## The two-product problem

This is where things get messy for Shopify teams.

If you want both customer support and CRM functionality — which most ecommerce businesses do — you need **two separate Freshworks products**. Freshdesk for tickets. Freshsales for customer data and sales pipelines.

That means:

- **Two subscriptions** with separate billing cycles
- **Two admin panels** to configure and maintain
- **Two sets of automation rules** that don't talk to each other
- **Separate user management** — your agents need seats in both products
- **Data silos** — customer context from your CRM doesn't automatically surface in support tickets without extra setup

Freshworks does offer some cross-product data sharing, but it's limited and requires manual configuration. The products were built as standalone tools first and stitched together later.

For a Shopify store owner, this matters. When a customer emails about a refund, your agent needs to see their order history, lifetime value, previous tickets, and CRM notes — all in one place. With Freshworks, that requires jumping between products or setting up custom integrations.

Auxx.ai was built as a single platform from day one. Your [CRM](/platform/crm) data, [ticket history](/platform/ticketing), and customer conversations all live in the same system. No stitching required.

## Where Freshworks falls short for Shopify

### Shallow Shopify integration

Freshworks has a native Shopify integration, but it's surface-level. You get basic order data displayed in ticket sidebars. Agents can view orders and process refunds from within Freshdesk. Multi-store support exists.

But that's about it. The integration doesn't extend to the CRM side — **Freshsales has no Shopify connection**. So your sales pipeline, customer segments, and lifecycle data are completely disconnected from your Shopify store.

There's no deep product catalog sync. No real-time inventory awareness. No customer lifetime value calculations pulled from actual Shopify data. And the AI can't reason over your Shopify data in any meaningful way because it doesn't have full access to it.

For general-purpose support teams, this might be fine. For a Shopify business where every ticket ties back to an order, a product, and a customer record in your store — it's a gap.

### AI costs add up fast

Freddy AI sounds great until you see the pricing.

- **Freddy AI Copilot**: extra **$29/agent/month** on top of your existing plan
- **Freddy AI Agent sessions**: 500 free sessions included, then **$49 per 100 additional sessions**

For a 5-agent team that wants copilot assistance, that's an extra $145/month just for the AI layer. If your AI agent handles any real volume, the session metering adds up quickly.

And here's the thing — AI in customer support isn't a nice-to-have anymore. It's table stakes. Metering AI sessions feels like metering how many times your agents can use search. It discourages the exact behavior you want to encourage.

Auxx.ai includes AI across the platform. You bring your own API keys (OpenAI, Anthropic, and others), so you pay the model providers directly at their rates. No markup. No per-session metering. No surprise bills.

### UI and usability complaints

Freshworks has gotten feedback about its interface. Long-time users report that the UI has gotten more cluttered over the years as features were bolted on. Navigation between products isn't seamless. The admin configuration can be overwhelming, especially when managing automation rules across both Freshdesk and Freshsales.

Their own customer support has also been a recurring complaint in reviews — which is ironic for a support software company.

### No self-hosting option

Freshworks is cloud-only. You can't self-host, you can't audit the source code, and your data lives on their infrastructure. For businesses with data residency requirements or teams that want full control over their support stack, this is a non-starter.

## Who should use Freshworks

Freshworks makes sense if you:

- Run a **large, general-purpose support operation** across multiple industries (not just ecommerce)
- Need **phone support** as a core channel (Freshcaller is mature)
- Already have a Freshworks stack and the cost of switching is high
- Need a **vendor with a long track record** and SOC 2, GDPR compliance out of the box
- Don't mind managing multiple products and the siloed experience
- Have the budget for AI add-ons without worrying about session metering

Freshworks is a safe, established choice for mid-market and enterprise teams that need a wide feature set and don't mind paying for it piece by piece.
