## You want a CRM. But which kind?

If you run a Shopify store and you've been googling "best CRM for e-commerce," Pipedrive probably showed up. It's popular, well-reviewed, and has a reputation for being easy to use.

But here's the thing — Pipedrive was built for B2B sales teams managing deal pipelines. Not for Shopify store owners handling customer support, order issues, and post-purchase communication.

Those are fundamentally different problems. And using the wrong tool for the job costs you more than the subscription fee. It costs you time, context, and the ability to actually help your customers.

Let's break down how Pipedrive and Auxx.ai compare, and when each one makes sense.

## Feature comparison

Here's a side-by-side look at what each platform offers:

| Feature | Pipedrive | Auxx.ai |
|---|---|---|
| **CRM** | Yes (sales pipeline) | Yes (customer support) |
| **Shopify integration** | No (requires Zapier/Make) | Native (orders, customers, products) |
| **Shared inbox** | No | Yes |
| **Ticketing** | No | Yes |

| **Free plan** | No | Yes |

The gap isn't subtle. These are two platforms built for completely different use cases.

## What Pipedrive does well

Credit where it's due — Pipedrive is genuinely good at what it was designed for.

### Visual pipeline management

Pipedrive's drag-and-drop deal pipeline is one of the best in the CRM space. You create stages, drag deals between them, and get a clear visual of where every opportunity sits. For a sales team tracking dozens of prospects through qualification, proposal, negotiation, and close stages, this is excellent.

The UI is clean and intuitive. Most people can start using it within minutes without reading documentation. That's a real achievement for a CRM.

### Ease of use

Pipedrive consistently ranks as one of the easiest CRMs to set up and learn. The onboarding is smooth, the interface is uncluttered, and it doesn't try to do everything at once. It does sales pipeline management and does it well.

### Integration ecosystem

With 500+ integrations and a solid API, Pipedrive connects to most business tools. Email sync with Gmail and Outlook works well. Calendar integration is reliable. If you need to plug it into your existing B2B sales workflow, you probably can.

### Activity-based selling

Pipedrive's approach of tracking activities (calls, emails, meetings) rather than just deal values is smart. It keeps sales reps focused on the actions that move deals forward rather than just staring at revenue numbers.

## Why Pipedrive doesn't fit Shopify support

Here's where things fall apart for e-commerce.

### No native Shopify integration

This is the biggest problem. Pipedrive has **no native Shopify integration**. If you want customer or order data in Pipedrive, you need to set up Zapier or Make workflows to sync it. That means a separate subscription, ongoing maintenance, and data that's always slightly out of date.

When a customer emails you about a wrong item, you can't pull up their order inside Pipedrive. You have to switch to Shopify admin, find the order, then switch back. That context-switching kills response times and frustrates your team.

With Auxx.ai, Shopify data is native. Orders, customers, products, fulfillment status — it's all there inside the platform. Your [CRM](/platform/crm) shows customer history alongside their Shopify orders automatically.

### No ticketing or shared inbox

Pipedrive doesn't have a help desk. There's no shared inbox. No ticket management. No way to assign customer issues to team members, track resolution times, or ensure nothing falls through the cracks.

For a sales CRM, that makes sense. Sales teams track deals, not support tickets. But for a Shopify store where 80% of customer communication is post-purchase support, the absence of [ticketing](/platform/ticketing) is a dealbreaker.

### No knowledge base

There's no way to build a customer-facing help center in Pipedrive. No FAQ pages, no article management, no self-service portal. Every question has to be answered manually.

A solid [knowledge base](/platform/knowledge-base) deflects a significant chunk of support volume. Customers find answers themselves. Your team handles fewer repetitive tickets. Pipedrive doesn't offer this at all.

### No live chat

Pipedrive doesn't include [live chat](/platform/live-chat) natively. You'd need a separate tool like Intercom or Drift, which means another subscription, another integration to maintain, and another place where customer context gets fragmented.

### No AI capabilities

There's no AI agent in Pipedrive that can read a customer's order, understand their issue, and draft a response. No AI that learns from your knowledge base and policies to handle common questions automatically.

For Shopify stores dealing with high volumes of repetitive questions — "where's my order," "how do I return this," "can I change my address" — AI support isn't a nice-to-have anymore. It's how you scale without hiring.

### It's a sales tool, not a support tool

This is the core issue. Pipedrive's entire architecture is built around the concept of a deal moving through a pipeline. That's the wrong mental model for customer support.

Support isn't a pipeline. It's a queue of conversations that need resolution. Customers don't move through "stages." They have problems that need to be solved, often within hours. The data model, the UI, the reporting — everything in Pipedrive is oriented around sales metrics, not support metrics like first response time, resolution rate, or customer satisfaction.

## Who should use Pipedrive

Pipedrive is a solid choice if:

- **You run a B2B company** with a dedicated sales team managing deal pipelines
- **Your revenue comes from outbound sales**, not e-commerce transactions
- **You don't need customer support tools** — you have a separate help desk already
- **You're tracking deals worth thousands of dollars** through multi-week or multi-month sales cycles
- **You have 10-50 employees** and need a clean, simple sales CRM

If that describes your business, Pipedrive is genuinely good at what it does. The visual pipeline, the activity tracking, and the ease of use make it a strong pick for sales-driven organizations.

But if you're a Shopify store, that probably doesn't describe your business.
