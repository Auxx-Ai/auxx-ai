## The AI support landscape is crowded

Every week there's a new AI tool claiming to revolutionize customer support. The reality is more nuanced. Most tools are good at one thing and weak at everything else. Choosing the right one depends on your stack, your volume, and how much control you want.

Here's what actually matters when evaluating AI support tools for e-commerce.

## What to look for

### 1. Native e-commerce integrations

The tool needs to connect to your store platform — not through a generic API, but with deep integration that understands orders, products, customers, and fulfillment status. A support AI that can't look up an order is just a fancy chatbot.

### 2. Multi-channel support

Your customers reach out via email, live chat, social media, and sometimes all three for the same issue. The AI should work across channels and maintain context between them.

### 3. Customizable AI behavior

You should be able to control the AI's tone, set boundaries on what it can and can't do, and define escalation rules. One-size-fits-all AI behavior doesn't work when every brand has different policies.

### 4. Transparent pricing

Some tools charge per resolution, per message, or per agent seat. Understand the pricing model before you commit — especially how costs scale as your volume grows.

## The honest truth

No AI tool is perfect. The best ones are transparent about their limitations and give you the controls to work around them. Look for tools that let you start small, measure results, and scale gradually.

The worst thing you can do is buy into a fully automated solution before you understand your support patterns. Start with AI-assisted responses, learn what works, then automate the patterns you trust.
