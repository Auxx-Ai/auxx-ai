## Response time is the #1 support metric

Customers don't just want good answers — they want them fast. Research consistently shows that response time is the single biggest factor in customer satisfaction for support interactions. A correct answer delivered 4 hours late is often worse than a partial answer delivered in 5 minutes.

For e-commerce businesses, the stakes are even higher. A slow response to a pre-sale question means a lost sale. A slow response to a shipping issue means a chargeback.

## Where the time actually goes

Before you can fix response times, you need to understand where the delays happen:

- **Triage** — figuring out what the ticket is about and who should handle it
- **Context gathering** — looking up the customer's order, history, and account details
- **Drafting** — writing the actual response
- **Review** — checking the response for accuracy before sending
- **Waiting** — tickets sitting in a queue because the team is busy

Most of these steps are either automatable or can be significantly accelerated.

## 5 strategies that actually work

### 1. Auto-categorize incoming tickets

Use AI to tag tickets by type (order status, returns, product questions, etc.) as they come in. This eliminates triage time and ensures tickets reach the right person immediately.

### 2. Surface relevant context automatically

When an agent opens a ticket, they should see the customer's order history, previous conversations, and account details without clicking through multiple tabs. This alone saves 2-3 minutes per ticket.

### 3. Use AI-drafted replies

AI can draft responses to common questions in seconds. The agent reviews, edits if needed, and sends. This cuts drafting time from 5 minutes to 30 seconds for routine tickets.

### 4. Create response templates for edge cases

Not everything can be automated. But you can create templates for your 20 most common edge cases, so agents aren't writing the same nuanced response from scratch every time.

### 5. Set up smart routing and priorities

VIP customers, urgent issues, and pre-sale questions should jump the queue. AI can assess priority based on the content and route accordingly.

## The compounding effect

These strategies don't just add up — they compound. When triage is instant, context is pre-loaded, and drafts are ready, your team can handle 3-4x the volume with the same headcount. That means fewer tickets waiting in the queue, which means even faster response times.

The result: response times drop from hours to minutes, and your team focuses on the cases that actually need human judgment.
