Every ticket can be assigned to a team member who is responsible for resolving it. Unassigned tickets show up in the dashboard's "Unassigned" count so nothing falls through the cracks.

## How assignment works

Each ticket has a single assignee — one team member responsible for it. The assignee is tracked as an Actor field, meaning it references a user in your organization.

When the assignee changes, Auxx.ai:
- Records the change in the ticket's activity timeline
- Publishes assignment events (useful for workflows and integrations)
- Updates the dashboard metrics

## Assign a ticket

### From the ticket detail view

1. Open a ticket
2. Click the **Assignee** field in the sidebar
3. Select a team member from the dropdown

The dropdown shows all members of your organization.

### From the ticket list

1. Select one or more tickets using the checkboxes
2. Click the **Assign** bulk action
3. Choose a team member

This is the fastest way to assign or reassign multiple tickets. See [bulk actions](/help/tickets/bulk-actions) for more.

## Remove an assignment

Click the assignee field and clear the selection, or set it to empty. The ticket returns to the unassigned pool.

## Automate assignment with workflows

For automatic routing, use [workflows](/help/ai/creating-workflows). You can create rules that automatically assign tickets based on conditions like:

- **Ticket type** — route refund tickets to your billing team
- **Priority** — assign urgent tickets to senior agents
- **Contact** — route VIP customers to a specific team member
- **Keywords** — assign tickets mentioning "shipping" to your logistics team

Workflows give you full control over routing logic without writing code.

## Tracking assignment changes

Every assignment change is logged in the ticket's activity feed with:
- Who made the change
- Who was assigned or unassigned
- When the change happened

This gives you a complete audit trail of ticket ownership over time.
