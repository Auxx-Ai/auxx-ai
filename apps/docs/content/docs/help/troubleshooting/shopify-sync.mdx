---
title: Shopify sync problems
description: Troubleshoot Shopify integration issues in Auxx.ai including connection failures, sync errors, and missing product or order data.
---

If your Shopify store isn't syncing correctly with Auxx.ai, use the steps below to diagnose and fix the issue.

## Connection issues

<Accordions type="single">
  <Accordion title="I can't connect my Shopify store">
    - Make sure you're entering the correct `.myshopify.com` domain
    - Verify you're logged into Shopify as the store owner or a staff member with app installation permissions — collaborator accounts may not have sufficient access
    - Check that your Shopify plan supports third-party app installations
    - If you previously connected and removed the store, try the connection again from **Settings > Shopify**
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="My Shopify connection shows as inactive">
    Go to **Settings > Shopify** and check the store status. If it shows as **Inactive**:

    - Click the toggle to re-enable the integration
    - If re-enabling fails, disconnect and reconnect the store
    - Your Shopify store may have revoked the app's access — check your Shopify admin under **Settings > Apps and sales channels**
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="OAuth authorization failed">
    - Clear your browser cookies and try again
    - Make sure pop-ups aren't blocked — the OAuth flow opens a Shopify authorization page
    - If you're using a VPN or firewall, it may be blocking the redirect. Try disabling temporarily.
  </Accordion>
</Accordions>

## Sync errors

<Accordions type="single">
  <Accordion title="Manual sync isn't working">
    From **Settings > Shopify**, try clicking the individual sync buttons (Products, Orders, Customers). If a sync job fails:

    - Check the sync job history for error details
    - Make sure the Shopify integration is set to **Active**
    - Verify your Shopify store is accessible (not paused or on a frozen plan)
    - Disconnect and reconnect the store to refresh the access token
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Sync is running but data isn't appearing">
    Sync jobs run as background processes. Large catalogs may take several minutes to complete.

    - Check the sync job status — it may still be in progress
    - Refresh the page after the sync completes
    - Navigate to the Shopify section in the sidebar to browse synced data
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Only some products/orders are syncing">
    - Shopify API pagination means large datasets are synced in batches — wait for the sync to fully complete
    - Draft or archived products in Shopify may not sync depending on their visibility settings
    - Check if there are Shopify API rate limits affecting the sync (visible in error logs)
  </Accordion>
</Accordions>

## Missing or outdated data

<Accordions type="single">
  <Accordion title="Changes in Shopify aren't reflected in Auxx.ai">
    Real-time updates rely on Shopify webhooks. If changes aren't appearing:

    - Webhooks may have been deregistered — disconnect and reconnect the store to re-register them
    - Try a manual sync to pull the latest data
    - Check that the integration is **Active** (inactive integrations pause webhook processing)
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Customer emails don't match contacts in Auxx.ai">
    Auxx.ai matches Shopify customers to contacts by email address. If a customer's email in Shopify differs from their contact email in Auxx.ai, they won't be automatically linked.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Deleted products still appear in Auxx.ai">
    When products are deleted in Shopify, a webhook notifies Auxx.ai. If the webhook was missed (e.g., during downtime), run a manual product sync to reconcile the data.
  </Accordion>
</Accordions>

## Multiple stores

<Accordions type="single">
  <Accordion title="I connected the wrong store">
    Go to **Settings > Shopify**, click **Remove** next to the incorrect store, then connect the correct one.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Data from different stores is mixing together">
    Each Shopify store connection is separate — products, orders, and customers are tagged with their source store. If data appears mixed, check that you're viewing the correct store's section.
  </Accordion>
</Accordions>
